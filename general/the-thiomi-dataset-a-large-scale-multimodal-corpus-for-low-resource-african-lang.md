# The Thiomi Dataset: A Large-Scale Multimodal Corpus for Low-Resource African Languages

The **Thiomi Dataset** represents a significant advancement in the field of [[Natural Language Processing]] specifically tailored for [[languages|low-resource languages]]. It is a large-scale, multimodal corpus designed to bridge the linguistic gap for ten African languages across four distinct language families.

### Dataset Composition
The corpus provides high-quality data for several key linguistic groups:
* **East Africa:** Swahili, Kikuyu, Kamba, Kimeru, Luo, Maasai, Kipsigis, and Somali.
* **West/Central Africa:** Wolof and Fulani.

The scale of the dataset is immense, containing over 601,000 approved sentence-level text annotations and over 385,000 audio recordings. By integrating both text and audio, the dataset serves as a foundation for training various [[a-comparative-analysis-of-machine-learning-models-in-shap-analysis|Machine Learning]] architectures, including [[Automatic Speech Recognition]] (ASR), [[Machine Translation]] (MT), and [[Text-to-Speech]] (TTS) models.

### Methodology and Data Collection
A primary innovation of the Thiomi project is its community-centric approach. Rather than relying solely on web-scraping, the researchers utilized a dedicated community data collection platform involving more than 100 active contributors. The study details specific quality assurance workflows designed to maintain high standards of linguistic accuracy across diverse dialects.

### Benchmarks and Performance
To demonstrate the dataset's efficacy, the authors established new baselines in [[artificial-intelligence-and-the-structure-of-mathematics|Artificial Intelligence]]-driven speech tasks. Most notably, the research achieved a breakthrough in Swahili ASR, reducing the Word Error Rate (WER) from a previous academic state-of-the-art of 8.3% down to 3.24%—a 61% relative reduction. Furthermore, the dataset enabled a 4.3% WER for Somali.

### Availability and Future Impact
The Thiomi Dataset is intended for public release via [[HuggingFace]], ensuring accessibility for the global research community. This work provides essential infrastructure for developing robust [[language technology]] and promotes more inclusive representation in global [[a-mathematical-theory-of-evolution-for-self-designing-ais|AI]] ecosystems.
