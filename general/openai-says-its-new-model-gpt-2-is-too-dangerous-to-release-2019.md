# OpenAI says its new model GPT-2 is too dangerous to release (2019)

In early 2019, the [[artificial-intelligence-and-the-structure-of-mathematics|Artificial Intelligence]] research organization [[openai-backs-illinois-bill-that-would-limit-when-ai-labs-can-be-held-liable|OpenAI]] announced a controversial decision regarding its latest breakthrough: the decision to withhold the full release of its highly capable language model, [[openai-says-its-new-model-gpt-2-is-too-dangerous-to-release-2019|GPT-2]]. While a scaled-down version was made available to the public, the complete, high-capacity version was kept under restricted access due to profound concerns regarding [[iatrobench-pre-registered-evidence-of-iatrogenic-harm-from-ai-safety-measures|AI Safety]] and the potential for large-scale harm.

## The Threat of Automated Disinformation
The primary driver behind this decision was the risk of generating human-like, deceptive text at an unprecedented scale. Researchers feared that the model's ability to produce coherent and convincing prose could be weaponized to automate [[Disinformation]] campaigns. In a digital landscape already vulnerable to "fake news," the prospect of using [[synthetic-trust-attacks-modeling-how-generative-ai-manipulates-human-decisions-i|Generative AI]] to flood social media with propaganda and manipulated content posed a significant threat to political stability and public trust.

## The Staged Release Approach
This event marked a critical shift in how organizations approach the deployment of [[a-comparative-analysis-of-machine-learning-models-in-shap-analysis|Machine Learning]] technologies. OpenAI adopted a "staged release" strategy, a method designed to allow researchers to study the risks and vulnerabilities of the model before making it widely accessible. This approach sparked an intense debate within the [[us-cities-are-axing-flock-safety-surveillance-technology|Technology]] community regarding the balance between [[battle-for-wesnoth-open-source-turn-based-strategy-game|Open Source]] transparency and the ethical responsibility to prevent misuse.

## Historical Significance
The 2019 GPT-2 controversy remains a landmark moment in the history of [[Natural Language Processing]]. It foreshadowed the current era of [[large-language-models-for-outpatient-referral-problem-definition-benchmarking-an|Large Language Models]] (LLMs), where the power of the models often outpaces our ability to regulate their outputs. The decision-making process used during this period continues to influence modern discussions around [[AI Ethics]] and the development of guardrails for increasingly autonomous systems.
