# RaPA: Enhancing Transferable Targeted Attacks via Random Parameter Pruning

The research paper introduces **RaPA** (Random Parameter Pruning Attack), a novel framework designed to bridge the performance gap in [[Targeted Adversarial Attacks]]. While significant progress has been made in improving [[Untargeted Attacks]] through methods like input diversification and gradient stabilization, achieving high [[Attack Success Rates]] (ASR) in targeted scenarios—where the goal is to force a model to classify an input into a specific incorrect category—remains a critical challenge in [[robust-ai-security-and-alignment-a-sisyphean-endeavor|AI Security]].

## The Problem: Parameter Over-reliance
A key discovery presented in this paper is that adversarial examples generated by current [[hybrid-fourier-neural-operator-for-surrogate-modeling-of-laser-processing-with-a|Surrogate Model]] methods depend too heavily on a small, specific subset of parameters within the surrogate architecture. This "over-reliance" creates a narrow optimization path that does not generalize well to unseen [[Target Models]], particularly when there is a significant structural difference between the source and target.

## The RaPA Solution
To address this, the authors propose the **Random Parameter Pruning Attack (RaPA)**. Instead of optimizing against a static model, RaPA introduces **parameter-level randomization** during the optimization process. 

At each optimization step, the algorithm randomly prunes specific model parameters. This creates a diverse ensemble of "sub-surrogate" variants that are visually and semantically consistent with the original model but possess different weight configurations. The authors mathematically demonstrate that this mechanism is functionally equivalent to implementing an **importance-equalization regularizer**, which prevents the attack from focusing on easily exploitable, model-specific features.

## Performance and Implementation
The effectiveness of RaPA was validated across both [[lipkernel-lipschitz-bounded-convolutional-neural-networks-via-dissipative-layers|Convolutional Neural Networks]] (CNN) and [[crft-consistent-recurrent-feature-flow-transformer-for-cross-modal-image-registr|Transformer]] architectures:

* **Cross-Architecture Transfer:** RaPA demonstrated exceptional performance in the most challenging scenarios, such as transferring attacks from CNN-based models to Transformer-based models, achieving up to an **11.7% increase** in average ASR over existing state-of-the-art baselines.
* **Deployment Efficiency:** The method is **training-free** and does not require the expensive re-training of surrogate models.
* **Integration:** Due to its simplicity, RaPA can be easily integrated into existing [[explainability-guided-adversarial-attacks-on-transformer-based-malware-detectors|Adversarial Attack]] frameworks as a plug-and-play optimization enhancement.
