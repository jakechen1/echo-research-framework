# Decisions and Deployment: The Five-Year SAHELI Project (2020-2025)

The **SAHELI Project** is a multi-year initiative (2020–2025) focused on improving [[Maternal and Child Health]] through the application of advanced [[artificial-intelligence-and-the-structure-of-mathematics|Artificial Intelligence]] to optimize resource distribution. Conducted in collaboration with the NGO ARMMAN, the project addresses the critical challenge of managing limited healthcare worker resources in India's public health programs.

## Overview of the Challenge
In many global health programs, the ability to provide personalized engagement is hindered by the scarcity of human resources. To maintain high levels of care, health programs must move away from static scheduling and toward dynamic, intelligent [[a-graph-foundation-model-for-wireless-resource-allocation|Resource Allocation]]. The core problem involves deciding when and how to deploy limited live-service resources to maximize long-term engagement with vulnerable beneficiaries.

## Methodological Innovation: From Prediction to Decision
The SAHELI project utilizes a [[Restless Multi-Armed Bandit]] (RMAB) framework to model the sequential decision-making process. A significant technical contribution of the project is the transition from a traditional two-stage "predict-then-optimize" approach to [[Decision-Focused Learning]] (DFL). 

In traditional models, the system first predicts a state and then optimizes based on that prediction, which often leads to errors when the prediction loss does not align with the decision loss. The DFL approach implemented in SAHELI directly integrates the learning objective with the ultimate goal of maximizing beneficiary engagement, effectively mitigating the "prediction-optimization gap."

## Impact and Results
The efficacy of the SAHELI system was evaluated through large-scale [[Randomized Controlled Trials]] (RCTs). The findings demonstrated that:
* **Engagement Stability:** The DFL-based policy reduced cumulative engagement drops by **31%** compared to the current standard of care.
* **Health Outcomes:** The increased engagement translated into statistically significant improvements in real-world health behaviors, most notably the sustained consumption of vital iron and calcium supplements among new mothers.

## Conclusion
The SAHELI project provides a scalable blueprint for integrating [[a-comparative-analysis-of-machine-learning-models-in-shap-analysis|Machine Learning]] into [[a-multi-agent-reinforcement-learning-framework-for-public-health-decision-analys|Public Health]] interventions. By demonstrating that [[a-firefly-algorithm-for-mixed-variable-optimization-based-on-hybrid-distance-mod|Algorithm]] design can directly improve clinical health outcomes, the project highlights the potential of sequential decision-making models to transform resource-constrained healthcare environments.
