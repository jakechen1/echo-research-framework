# Cambodia Unveils Statue to Honour Heroic Landmine-Sniffing Rat

In a significant gesture of gratitude toward the non-human contributors to [[humanitarian demining]], Cambodia has unveiled a commemorative statue to honor a famous rat trained to detect [[landmines]]. The monument serves as a lasting tribute to the vital role played by specialized animals in clearing explosive remnants of war from much-contested territories.

The program, notably led by the organization [[APOPO]], utilizes [[African Giant Pouched Rats]] for explosive detection. These animals are chosen specifically for their highly developed [[olfactory system]], which allows them to identify the chemical signatures of TNT and other explosives with extreme precision. Unlike heavy machinery, these rats are light enough to traverse mine-strewn fields without triggering pressure-sensitive detonators, making them a safer and more efficient alternative in many complex terrains.

This method represents a unique application of [[neurobiology|biology]] in the field of [[disaster response]]. While advancements in [[us-cities-are-axing-flock-safety-surveillance-technology|technology]] and [[ai-driven-marine-robotics-emerging-trends-in-underwater-perception-and-ecosystem|robotics]] continue to evolve, the use of trained biological agents provides a cost-effective and high-accuracy solution for [[landmine removal]] in developing nations. The success of these "hero rats" has paved the way for broader discussions on how animal intelligence can complement modern [[a-comparative-analysis-of-machine-learning-models-in-shap-analysis|machine-learning]] and sensor-based detection methods.
