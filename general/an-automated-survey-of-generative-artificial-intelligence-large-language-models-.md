# An Automated Survey of Generative Artificial Intelligence

This paper provides a comprehensive and automated overview of the rapidly evolving landscape of [[an-automated-survey-of-generative-artificial-intelligence-large-language-models-|Generative Artificial Intelligence]] as of early 2026. The survey serves as a longitudinal study of the transition from early [[large-language-models-for-outpatient-referral-problem-definition-benchmarking-an|Large Language Models]] (LLMs) to the frontier architectures that define the current era of [[a-comparative-analysis-of-machine-learning-models-in-shap-analysis|Machine Learning]].

## Model Analysis and Architectures

A central contribution of this work is the detailed comparative analysis of both open-weight and proprietary model families. The authors evaluate the technical innovations and training regimes of several prominent systems, including:

* **Open-Weight Systems:** Significant focus is placed on the [[DeepSeek]] lineage (including DeepSeek-V3, R1, and the anticipated V4), the [[Qwen]] series, [[LLaMA 4]], [[Mistral Large 3]], and smaller efficient models like [[Gemma 3]] and [[Phi-4]].
* **Proprietary Systems:** The survey assesses the performance of industry leaders such as [[GPT-5.4]], [[Gemini 3.1 Pro]], [[claude|Claude Opus 4.6]], and [[Grok 4.20]].

Performance metrics are derived from empirical benchmarks and real-world user preference data from the [[Chatbot Arena]] leaderboard.

## Deployment Protocols and Agentic Ecosystems

The survey moves beyond static model evaluation to examine the protocols that facilitate [[undetectable-conversations-between-ai-agents-via-pseudorandom-noise-resilient-ke|AI Agents]] and interoperability. Key research areas discussed include:

* [[Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)]] for enhancing context accuracy.
* The [[Model Context Protocol (MCP)]] for standardized data interface.
* [[Agent-to-Agent protocols]] and standardized [[function calling]] frameworks.
* Modern [[serving frameworks]] for scalable deployment.

## Real-World Applications

The research demonstrates the impact of generative technology across fifteen distinct industry sectors. Case studies highlight the integration of AI in [[Financial Services]], [[Legal Technology]], [[precision-agriculture|Agriculture]], and [[Tourism]], showcasing the shift from experimental use to core operational infrastructure.
