# I still prefer MCP over skills

In the rapidly advancing field of [[artificial-intelligence-and-the-structure-of-mathematics|Artificial Intelligence]], a fundamental architectural debate has emerged: should AI agents rely on bespoke "skills," or should they utilize a standardized protocol like the [[Model Context Protocol]] (MCP)?

The article "I still prefer MCP over skills" posits that the industry is at a crossroads between fragmented, custom-integrated toolsets and a unified, interoperable ecosystem. "Skills" refers to the traditional method of programming specific capabilities or unique tool-calling functions directly into an [[analyzing-multimodal-interaction-strategies-for-llm-assisted-manipulation-of-3d-|LLM]] agent.
