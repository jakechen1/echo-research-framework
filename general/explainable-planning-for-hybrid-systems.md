# Explainable Planning for Hybrid Systems

The rapid advancement of [[Artificial Intelligence]] (AI) technologies is driving a fundamental paradigm shift toward widespread [[Automation]]. As [[Autonomous Systems]] increasingly replace manually operated processes, the ability to govern and understand their decision-making becomes paramount. At the heart of these autonomous agents lies [[Automated Planning]], the computational process of generating a sequence of actions to achieve specific goals.

As powerful planners are integrated into [[Safety-Critical Domains]], the stakes of decision-making rise significantly. These domains include highly sensitive infrastructures such as [[Smart Energy Grids]], [[Self-Driving Cars]], and [[Air Traffic Control]], as well as high-stakes operational environments like [[Healthcare]], [[Search and Rescue]], and [[Warehouse Automation]]. While these technologies offer unprecedented efficiency, the "black box" nature of complex planning algorithms presents a significant barrier to human trust and operational safety.

A major frontier in the [[Planning Community]] is the development of [[Explainable Artificial Intelligence Planning]] (XAIP). There is an urgent necessity to generate human-understandable explanations that justify why a specific plan was chosen over alternatives. This research, detailed in arXiv:2604.09578, focuses specifically on the challenges of providing explanations within [[Hybrid Systems]].

[[Hybrid Systems]] are critical for real-world applications because they allow for a more high-fidelity representation of physical reality, blending discrete logical transitions with continuous physical dynamics (such as motion, temperature, or energy consumption). By providing a comprehensive study on XAIP for these hybrid models, this work aims to bridge the gap between complex computational logic and human interpretability, ensuring that the next generation of [[Robotics]] and autonomous infrastructure is both efficient and transparent.
