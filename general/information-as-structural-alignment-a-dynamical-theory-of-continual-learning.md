# Information as Structural Alignment: A Dynamical Theory of Continual Learning

The paper "Information as Structural Alignment: A Dynamical Theory of Continual Learning" proposes a fundamental shift in how we approach [[Catastrophic Forgetting]]. Rather than treating forgetting as a mere engineering flaw, the authors argue that it is an inevitable mathematical consequence of storing knowledge through [[Global Parameter Superposition]].

To resolve this, the paper introduces the [[Informational Buildup Framework]] (IBF). In this framework, information is not viewed as stored content, but as the achievement of "structural alignment." The system's evolution is governed by two primary dynamics:

1.  **Law of Motion:** A mechanism that drives the system configuration toward higher levels of [[a-unified-stability-analysis-of-sam-vs-sgd-role-of-data-coherence-and-emergence-|Coherence]].
2.  **Modification Dynamics:** A process that persistently deforms the [[Coherence Landscape]] in response to localized discrepancies.
