# Integrating Artificial Intelligence, Physics, and Internet of Things: A Framework for Cultural Heritage Conservation

The paper "Integrating Artificial Intelligence, Physics, and Internet of Things: A Framework for Cultural Heritage Conservation" introduces a multi-disciplinary approach to the preservation of historical assets. As cultural heritage faces increasing threats from environmental shifts and material decay, this framework proposes a synergy between [[artificial-intelligence-and-the-structure-of-mathematics|Artificial Intelligence]], the [[integrating-artificial-intelligence-physics-and-internet-of-things-a-framework-f|Internet of Things]] (IoT), and physical domain expertise to enable effective monitoring and predictive maintenance.

### Framework Architecture
The proposed framework is organized into four functional layers designed to analyze 3D models of cultural assets and execute elaborate simulations. A central innovation is the implementation of [[machine-learning|Scientific Machine Learning]], specifically [[curvature-aware-optimization-for-high-accuracy-physics-informed-neural-networks|Physics-Informed Neural Networks]] (PINNs). Unlike traditional black-box AI, PINNs incorporate fundamental physical laws directly into deep learning models. This ensures that the simulations regarding structural integrity and degradation remain physically consistent with real-world phenomena.

### Computational Efficiency and Modeling
To address the immense computational demands of simulating complex, real-life geometries, the framework integrates [[Reduced Order Methods]] (ROMs), such as [[Proper Orthogonal Decomposition]] (POD). The system remains compatible with classical [[Finite Element]] (FE) methods, allowing for a hybrid approach to structural analysis. This integration enables the efficient modeling of degradation processes influenced by various environmental and material parameters.

Furthermore, the framework includes automated tools for managing 3D digital replicas, enabling the creation of high-fidelity [[graph-neural-ode-digital-twins-for-control-oriented-reactor-thermal-hydraulic-fo|Digital Twins]] for cultural sites. This allows for the processing of complex 3D models into formats suitable for reliable large-scale simulation.

### Key Contributions
The research outlines three primary contributions to the field of [[Cultural Heritage Preservation]]:
1. A specialized methodology for processing 3D models of cultural assets for reliable simulation.
2. The application of PINNs to bridge the gap between data-driven and physics-based approaches.
3. The integration of PINNs with ROMs to model degradation processes with high computational efficiency.

The experimental phase utilizes both simulated and real-life complex geometries to test the framework's efficacy in solving both direct and inverse problems.
