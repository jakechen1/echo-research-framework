# A Rosetta Stone Hypothesis for Neurophenomenology

A fundamental challenge in [[Consciousness Science]] is the "explanatory gap" between first-person subjective experiences and third-person empirical observations. While [[a-rosetta-stone-hypothesis-for-neurophenomenology-mathematical-predictions-from-|Neurophenomenology]] aims to bridge this divide by creating "generative passages" between experience and neuroscientific data, the field has lacked the robust mathematical machinery required to formalize these connections.

## The Rosetta Stone Hypothesis

This paper proposes a "**Rosetta Stone hypothesis**" derived from the framework of [[Predictive Processing]]. The central premise is that [[avoiding-non-integrable-beliefs-in-expectation-propagation|Beliefs]] act as a mathematical hub, providing a unified link between three critical domains:
1.  **Phenomenology** (the content of subjective experience)
2.  **Behavior** (observable physical actions)
3.  **Neural Dynamics** (the underlying biological activity)

The hypothesis hinges on a pivotal technical assumption: that phenomenology is a direct function of internal beliefs. By utilizing a conditional approach, the researchers demonstrate that if this assumption is correct, several specific, measurable predictions must mathematically follow.

## Mathematical Predictions

The authors derive specific frameworks that can be empirically tested to validate the link between mental states and brain activity. These predictions include:

*   **Subjective Similarity Judgments**: How the brain calculates the closeness of different experiences.
*   **Cognitive Metabolic Cost**: The relationship between the complexity of information processing and energy expenditure.
*   **Subjective Cognitive Effort**: The mathematical modeling of perceived mental strain.
*   **Time Perception**: The derivation of how temporal duration is subjectively experienced.
