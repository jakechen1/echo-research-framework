# Project Glasswing: Securing critical software for the AI era

## Overview
Project Glasswing is a research-driven initiative focused on the intersection of [[triage-routing-software-engineering-tasks-to-cost-effective-llm-tiers-via-code-q|Software Engineering]] and [[artificial-intelligence-and-the-structure-of-mathematics|Artificial Intelligence]] safety. As the integration of [[large-language-models-for-outpatient-referral-problem-definition-benchmarking-an|Large Language Models]] into the software development lifecycle becomes more pervasive, the project seeks to address the emerging security landscape where AI-driven capabilities meet the fundamental layers of digital infrastructure.

## The Challenge of the AI Era
The transition toward an era of autonomous coding introduces unprecedented risks to [[security|Cybersecurity]]. As [[a-comparative-analysis-of-machine-learning-models-in-shap-analysis|Machine Learning]] models gain the ability to write, debug, and deploy code, the traditional boundaries of the [[Software Supply Chain]] are shifting. There is a growing concern that the speed of AI-assisted development may outpace our ability to perform rigorous [[Software Verification]], potentially introducing subtle, high-impact vulnerabilities into [[Critical Infrastructure]].

Furthermore, the potential for [[Automated Exploit Generation]]—where AI is used to discover and weaponize zero-day vulnerabilities—necessitates a proactive approach to defending the software that underpins global digital ecosystems.

## Core Research Objectives
Project Glasswing focuses on several key pillars of protection:
* **Resilient Infrastructure:** Developing methods to secure the foundational software layers that support both human and [[artificial-intelligence-and-the-structure-of-mathematics|Artificial Intelligence]] operations.
* **Automated Defense:** Enhancing [[Automated Testing]] and [[automated-conjecture-resolution-with-formal-verification|Formal Verification]] techniques to detect flaws that might be obscured by AI-generated complexity.
* **Risk Mitigation:** Identifying the specific failure modes introduced when [[automating-database-native-function-code-synthesis-with-llms|LLMs]] are granted agency over critical software components.

## Significance
By prioritizing the security of the "glasswing" layers—the transparent but vital software components that hold systems together—this initiative contributes to the broader field of [[Safety Engineering]]. Project Glasswing emphasizes that [[iatrobench-pre-registered-evidence-of-iatrogenic-harm-from-ai-safety-measures|AI Safety]] cannot be achieved in isolation; it requires a holistic commitment to securing the broader [[us-cities-are-axing-flock-safety-surveillance-technology|Technology]] ecosystem and the code that defines it.
