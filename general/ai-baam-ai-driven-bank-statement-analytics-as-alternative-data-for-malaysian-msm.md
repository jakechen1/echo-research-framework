# AI-BAAM: AI-Driven Bank Statement Analytics

**AI-BAAM** is an innovative [[a-comparative-analysis-of-machine-learning-models-in-shap-analysis|Machine Learning]] framework designed to address the credit accessibility gap for [[Micro, Small, and Medium Enterprises (MSMEs)]] in [[ai-baam-ai-driven-bank-statement-analytics-as-alternative-data-for-malaysian-msm|Malaysia]]. Despite representing 96.1% of all businesses in the country, MSMEs—particularly new ventures—are frequently excluded from formal credit markets. This exclusion stems from the limitations of traditional underwriting, which relies heavily on historical [[Credit Bureau]] data that may not reflect the current liquidity of a young business.

## The AI-BAAM Framework

The study proposes a specialized, cash-flow-based underwriting pipeline. Unlike traditional methods, AI-BAAM utilizes [[ai-baam-ai-driven-bank-statement-analytics-as-alternative-data-for-malaysian-msm|Alternative Data]] extracted directly from bank statements to create a more granular view of a business's financial health. The pipeline consists of two primary stages:

1.  **End-to-End Data Extraction:** Utilizing [[artificial-intelligence-and-the-structure-of-mathematics|Artificial Intelligence]] to automate the extraction of structured information from unstructured bank transaction records.
2.  **Predictive Modeling:** Developing [[Credit Scoring]] models that leverage transaction-derived features to assess the probability of default.

To evaluate the system, the researchers introduced a novel dataset containing 611 loan applicants from a Malaysian consulting firm.

## Empirical Results

The integration of bank statement features significantly enhances the predictive accuracy of the underwriting process. The study demonstrated that:

*   The best-performing model achieved an **AUROC of 0.806** on the validation set.
*   This represents a **24.6% improvement** over baseline models that rely solely on standard application information.

## Significance and Future Research

The development of AI-BAAM is a critical step toward promoting [[Financial Inclusion]] in emerging economies. By leveraging [[us-cities-are-axing-flock-safety-surveillance-technology|Technology]] to unlock the value of transaction data, the framework provides a pathway for unbanked or underbanked MSMEs to access formal financing. To encourage further advancement in the field, the authors have committed to releasing an anonymized version of the bank transaction dataset to facilitate global research into economic stability and [[a-comparative-analysis-of-machine-learning-models-in-shap-analysis|Machine Learning]] applications in finance.
