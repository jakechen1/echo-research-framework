# I won't download your app. The web version is a-ok

The fundamental barrier to user acquisition in modern [[triage-routing-software-engineering-tasks-to-cost-effective-llm-tiers-via-code-q|Software Engineering]] is often not the lack of features, but the level of friction required to access them. The article "I won't download your app. The web version is a-ok" explores the growing psychological and technical resistance users feel toward installing native applications in favor of the frictionless experience provided by the [[web-browser|Web Browser]].

### The Friction of Installation

Every time a user is asked to download a native application, they are asked to make a series of micro-commitments that increase the "cost" of the software:

*   **Resource Allocation:** The user must allocate physical [[storage-space|Storage Space]] on their device.
*   **Security Hurdles:** The user must navigate [[system-permissions|System Permissions]], often granting access to contacts, location, or camera.
*   **Maintenance Overhead:** The user becomes responsible for managing [[software-updates|Software Updates]] and ensuring the app remains compatible with their [[rhizome-os-1-rhizomes-semi-autonomous-operating-system-for-small-molecule-drug-d|Operating System]].

In the context of [[mobile-ecosystems|Mobile Ecosystems]], this friction is a primary driver of user churn. If a tool is only needed occasionally, the cognitive load of a permanent installation outweighs the utility of the service.

### The Advantage of the Web

Conversely, the web provides a "zero-install" entry point. Through the use of [[hyperlinks|Hyperlinks]], a user can transition from discovery to utility in milliseconds. The gap between native performance and web performance is rapidly closing due to advancements in [[webassembly|WebAssembly]] and highly optimized [[javascript|JavaScript]] engines. 

This shift has significant implications for [[product-management|Product Management]] and [[saas|SaaS]] development. By prioritizing a "web-first" or "web-only" strategy, developers can reduce the barrier to entry, leverage the ubiquity of the [[cloud-computing|Cloud Computing]] model, and capture users who prioritize immediate access over deep hardware integration. As [[user-experience|User Experience]] (UX) continues to evolve, the ability to deliver value without the burden of a download remains a critical competitive advantage in the broader [[us-cities-are-axing-flock-safety-surveillance-technology|Technology]] landscape.
