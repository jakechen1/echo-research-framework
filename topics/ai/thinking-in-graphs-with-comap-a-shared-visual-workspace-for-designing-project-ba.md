# Thinking in Graphs with CoMAP

The paper "Thinking in Graphs with CoMAP" introduces a novel computational framework designed to facilitate the complex, non-linear process of designing [[project-based-learning|Project-Based Learning]] (PBL). Traditional design workflows often rely on linear documentation or purely conversational [[artificial-intelligence-and-the-structure-of-mathematics|Artificial Intelligence]], both of which struggle to maintain the high level of interdependence required for creative instructional design.

## The CoMAP Framework

CoMAP (Collaborative Map) is built upon the principles of [[distributed-cognition|Distributed Cognition]]. Unlike standard chat-based interfaces that operate in a "prompt-and-response" loop, CoMAP provides a shared, graph-based visual workspace. This interface enables a dual-modality AI support system that allows users to manipulate and visualize complex relationships between different educational components.

By shifting the focus from dialogue to a persistent, shared artifact, CoMAP enables a more transparent and equitable [[human-ai-collaboration|Human-AI Collaboration]]. The system allows educators to visualize the connections between learning objectives, activities, and assessments as a networked structure of nodes and edges, rather than a static, disconnected list.

## Empirical Findings

A study involving 30 educators was conducted to evaluate the effectiveness of CoMAP against a standard dialogue-only baseline. The research demonstrated that the graph-based approach provided several significant benefits:

* **Design Expression:** Educators were able to more accurately represent their complex instructional visions.
* **Divergent Thinking:** The visual interface encouraged more creative and varied instructional paths.
* **Iterative Practice:** The workspace facilitated easier prototyping and rapid refinement of lesson plans.

Furthermore, the system was found to reduce the [[cognitive-load|Cognitive Load]] associated with managing multi-faceted design components. By providing a persistent context, CoMAP fosters greater trust in the AI partner and allows educators to maintain greater agency and control over their creative processes.
