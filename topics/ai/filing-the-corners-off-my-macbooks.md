# Filing the corners off my MacBooks

The article "Filing the corners off my MacBooks" explores an extreme and highly personal form of [[hardware hacking]]. Rather than focusing on software optimizations or internal component upgrades, the author details a physical modification of the device's aluminum chassis: manually filing down the sharp, precision-engineered corners of the MacBook hardware.

## Motivation and Ergonomics

The primary driver behind this modification appears to be [[ergonomics]] and the tactile experience of [[personal computing]]. Modern laptop designs, particularly those from Apple, prioritize thin profiles and sharp, clean lines. While aesthetically pleasing in an [[industrial design]] context, these edges can sometimes catch on clothing or provide a less comfortable tactile sensation during extended use. By smoothing these edges, the author seeks to transform the physical interface between the human hand and the [[technology]], making the device feel more "organic" and less intrusive.

## Risks and Implications

Such a permanent alteration to [[consumer electronics]] carries significant implications:

*   **Warranty and Support:** Any permanent physical modification to the chassis effectively voids the manufacturer's [[warranty]] and complicates any potential repair through official channels.
*   **Structural Integrity:** Altering the geometry of the aluminum casing raises questions about the long-term [[structural integrity]] of the device and how the chassis handles stress during transport.
*   **Aesthetics vs. Utility:** There is a tension between maintaining the pristine, intended [[aesthetic]] of the hardware and the utility gained through personalized [[tinkering]].

The discourse surrounding this modification, particularly on platforms like [[Hacker News]], highlights the divide between users who view devices as pristine, untouchable objects and those who view them as malleable tools subject to [[hardware modification]] for personal comfort. This practice serves as a unique case study in how individuals can reclaim the physical form of their tools to better suit their specific physical needs.
