# PhySe-RPO

**PhySe-RPO** (Physics and Semantics Guided Relative Policy Optimization) is an advanced [[Diffusion Model]] framework designed to address the critical challenge of surgical smoke obscuration in [[Robotic Surgery]]. During intraoperative procedures, smoke generated by cautery tools can severely degrade video quality, obscuring anatomical structures and limiting the surgeon's perception of the surgical field.

### The Problem: Deterministic Limitations
Traditional [[Computer Vision]] approaches to "desmoking" typically rely on deterministic restoration pipelines and large-scale, paired training datasets (clean vs. smoky images). However, obtaining such paired supervision in real clinical environments is extremely difficult. Furthermore, deterministic models lack the ability to perform exploration or refinement under the unpredictable-conditions of live surgery.

### The Solution: Stochastic Policy Optimization
PhySe-RPO transforms the deterministic restoration task into a stochastic policy. By utilizing **Relative Policy Optimization (RPO)**, the framework enables trajectory-level exploration and allows for critic-free updates through group-relative optimization. This shift allows the model to iteratively refine the desmoking process without requiring a direct ground-truth reference for every frame.

### Dual-Reward Architecture
The innovation of PhySe-RPO lies in its two-pronged reward system, which ensures that the restored images are both physically accurate and anatomically meaningful:

*   **Physics-Guided Reward**: This component imposes strict constraints on illumination and color consistency. It ensures that the de-smoking process does not introduce artifacts or artificial color shifts, maintaining the physical integrity of the surgical lighting.
*   **Semantics-Guided Reward**: Utilizing [[CLIP]]-based surgical concepts, this reward promotes "anatomical coherence." It rewards the model when the output aligns with known surgical semantic features, ensuring that reconstructed tissues and structures are recognizable and medically plausible.

### Clinical Significance
By combining these rewards with a reference-free perceptual constraint, PhySe-RPO demonstrates high performance across both synthetic and real-world robotic surgical datasets. This approach provides a principled, robust method for [[Deep Learning]] applications in [[Medical Imaging]], specifically providing a route to high-fidelity restoration even when paired supervision is scarce.
