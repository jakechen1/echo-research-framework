# We've raised $17M to build what comes after Git

GitButler, an ambitious project aimed at revolutionizing [[Version Control]], has announced the completion of a $17 million [[Series A]] funding round. The capital is earmarked for the development of a new paradigm in software configuration management, specifically designed to evolve beyond the current limitations inherent in the [[Git]] ecosystem.

The core philosophy behind GitButler is to mitigate the cognitive load placed on developers by traditional branching models. In modern [[Software Development]], managing complex merge conflicts and tracking disparate feature branches can become a major impediment to engineering velocity. The team focuses on a workflow that emphasizes "changesets" rather than the rigid, often cumbersome structures of standard Git branches. This approach aims to simplify the developer experience, making it easier to work on multiple tasks simultaneously without the fear of tangled histories or complex rebasing operations.

The expansion of the engineering team will focus on scaling the underlying technology to handle massive repositories and highly distributed teams. As part of the broader [[Technology]] sector's movement toward more-intelligent developer tools, GitButler's approach represents a significant shift toward automation and abstraction in the [[DevOps]] lifecycle.
