# France Launches Government Linux Desktop Plan as Windows Exit Begins

The French government has initiated a strategic roadmap focused on strengthening [[digital-sovereignty|Digital Sovereignty]] by reducing the public sector's reliance on non-European software ecosystems. A central pillar of this plan involves transitioning administrative desktop environments from [[microsoft-windows|Microsoft Windows]] toward [[france-launches-government-linux-desktop-plan-as-windows-exit-begins|Linux]]-based solutions.

The primary driver behind this transition is the mitigation of [[extra-european|Extra-European]] technological dependencies. By moving away from proprietary software controlled by external corporations, the French state aims to mitigate risks associated with [[cybersecurity|Cybersecurity]], data privacy, and unilateral software changes that could affect critical national infrastructure. This shift is part of a broader movement toward ensuring that the digital tools used by the state are auditable, transparent, and under domestic or European control.

The adoption of [[battle-for-wesnoth-open-source-turn-based-strategy-game|Open Source]] software is viewed as a critical component of this strategy. Unlike proprietary systems, [[battle-for-wesnoth-open-source-turn-based-strategy-game|Open Source]] platforms allow for greater interoperability and the ability for local developers to contribute to the security and functionality of the [[rhizome-os-1-rhizomes-semi-autonomous-operating-system-for-small-molecule-drug-d|Operating System]]. This not only fosters a more resilient digital landscape but also encourages the growth of a domestic [[us-cities-are-axing-flock-safety-surveillance-technology|Technology]] sector capable of sustaining high-level digital needs.

As the "Windows exit" begins, the implications extend beyond hardware; it represents a fundamental change in how the state manages its digital assets and information technology. The success of this plan will depend on the seamless migration of essential administrative tools and the ability of the state to maintain service continuity while phasing out legacy systems in favor of a more autonomous and secure [[digital-infrastructure|Digital Infrastructure]].
