# Causal Stance

The **Causal Stance** is a conceptual framework introduced to resolve fundamental ambiguities in the [[philosophy-of-mind|philosophy of mind]], specifically regarding the debate over [[physical-causal-closure|physical causal closure]]. The framework proposes a necessary distinction between two different descriptive modes: the **Physical Stemma/Stance** and the **Causal Stance**.

## The Dual-Stance Framework

Traditional arguments often conflate physical causation with deterministic physical lawfulness. This paper argues that such an identification is a category error. To clarify the relationship between physical laws and causal effects, the study defines:

*   **The Physical Stance**: A mode of description centered on physical lawfulness and [[physical-determinism|physical determinism]]. In this stance, the state of the universe is governed by fixed, deterministic laws.
*   **The Causal Stance**: A mode of description centered on the asymmetry between cause and effect. This stance is grounded in [[manipulability]] and [[analysis-of-non-pharmaceutical-interventions-with-sir-epidemic-models-decreasing|intervention]], principles that are foundational to contemporary scientific practice and the ability to observe causal relationships through experimentation.

## Philosophical Implications

The paper critiques the influential position of [[jaegwon-kim|Jaegwon Kim]], who equates physical causation with physical lawfulness. By doing so, Kim’s model suggests that physical causation is inherently tied to determinism. The author argues that because causation is not explicitly defined within the language of [[neural-assistive-impulses-synthesizing-exaggerated-motions-for-physics-based-cha|physics]], "physical causal closure" cannot be a concept belonging solely to the Physical Stance.

By decoupling these two stances, the paper provides a reconstruction of [[donald-davidson|Donald Davidson]]'s [[anomalous-monism|Anomalous Monism]]. This reconstruction presents a consistent materialist position where mental causation can be acknowledged without contradicting the deterministic nature of the Physical Stance. 

## Scientific Relevance

The development of the **Dual-Laws Model** is presented as a requirement for the future of [[neuroscience]] and the study of [[consciousness]]. By constructing a linguistic and conceptual framework where physical causal closure does not hold within the Causal Stance—even while physical determinism remains intact in the Physical Stance—scientists can better model complex biological and mental phenomena without violating the established laws of [[neural-assistive-impulses-synthesizing-exaggerated-motions-for-physics-based-cha|physics]].
