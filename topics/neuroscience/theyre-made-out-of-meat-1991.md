# They're made out of meat (1991)

"They're made out of meat" is a celebrated short story authored by [[terry-bisson|Terry Bisson]], first published in 1991. The work is a cornerstone of speculative [[science-fiction|Science Fiction]], famously utilizing a dialogue-driven narrative to explore the concept of [[first-contact|First Contact]] through a lens of profound ontological shock.

### Narrative Summary
The story is presented as a conversation between extraterrestrial entities who are grappling with the startling discovery of humanity. The central conflict arises from the biological nature of human beings. To these extraterrestrials, the idea that a sentient, technologically capable species could be composed entirely of organic, carbon-based "meat" is both incomprehensible and deeply unsettling. The dialogue emphasizes the visceral reaction to finding intelligence within such a messy, decaying, and biological substrate.

### Themes and Philosophical Implications
Thematically, the work intersects significantly with contemporary discussions in [[artificial-intelligence-and-the-structure-of-mathematics|Artificial Intelligence]] and [[neuroscience|Neuroscience]]. It serves as a profound thought experiment regarding **substrate independence**—the theory that [[artificial-intelligence-and-the-structure-of-mathematics|Intelligence]] and consciousness are not tied to a specific physical medium. 

The characters in the story appear to represent a form of [[digital-life|Digital Life]] or [[silicon-based-intelligence|Silicon-based Intelligence]], viewing the human reliance on [[neurobiology|Biology]] as a primitive or even grotesque way to host complex thought. This mirrors modern debates in [[a-comparative-analysis-of-machine-learning-models-in-shap-analysis|Machine Learning]] and [[post-humanism|Post-humanism]] regarding whether mind is a pattern that can be transferred between different types of hardware, or if [[sentience|Sentience]] is inextricably linked to the organic processes of the brain.

As we continue to develop more sophisticated [[curvature-aware-optimization-for-high-accuracy-physics-informed-neural-networks|Neural Networks]] and explore the boundaries of [[synthetic-biology|Synthetic Biology]], Bisson's work remains a highly relevant critique of how we define "life" and "intelligence" in an era of increasing distinction between the biological and the technological.
