# Minimaxity and Admissibility of Bayesian Neural Networks

The paper "Minimaxity and Admissibility of Bayesian Neural Networks" investigates a critical gap in the intersection of [[statistical-decision-theory|Statistical Decision Theory]] and deep learning. While [[bayesian-neural-networks-an-introduction-and-survey|Bayesian Neural Networks]] (BNNs) are a cornerstone of modern [[artificial-intelligence-and-the-structure-of-mathematics|Artificial Intelligence]] due to their ability to provide principled probabilistic uncertainty, their theoretical optimality properties—specifically regarding minimaxity and admissibility—have received limited attention in the context of deep, non-linear architectures.
