# Every GPU That Mattered

"Every GPU That Mattered" is an extensive data visualization project that chronicles the historical trajectory of the Graphics Processing Unit (GPU). The project serves as a visual archive, mapping the performance, architecture, and release milestones of the most influential graphics processors that have shaped the landscape of [[modern-computing|modern computing]].

The visualization highlights how the progression of hardware has moved far beyond mere pixel manipulation for gaming into the realm of high-performance [[parallel-computing|parallel computing]]. As the density of transistors increased through advances in [[semiconductor]] manufacturing, GPUs evolved into versatile engines capable of handling massive mathematical workloads. This evolution was a critical precursor to the current era of [[artificial-intelligence-and-the-structure-of-mathematics|Artificial Intelligence]], as the ability to perform simultaneous operations became the essential backbone for [[curvature-aware-optimization-for-high-accuracy-physics-informed-neural-networks|neural networks]] and [[benchmarking-deep-learning-for-future-liver-remnant-segmentation-in-colorectal-l|deep learning]]-based models.

Key industry players, most notably [[nvidia-greenboost-transparently-extend-gpu-vram-using-system-ramnvme|NVIDIA]], are featured through their significant architectural iterations. The transition from fixed-function pipelines to programmable shaders represents a fundamental shift in how developers approach [[graphics-programming|graphics programming]]. Furthermore, the collection contextualizes the importance of the [[gpgpu|GPGPU]] (General-Purpose computing on Graphics Processing Units) movement, which allowed researchers to repurpose gaming hardware for [[scientific-computing|scientific computing]] and complex simulations in [[computational-biology|computational biology]].

The project has sparked significant discussion in technical communities such as [[hacker-news|Hacker News]], where users analyze the correlation between GPU architectural leaps and the acceleration of [[a-comparative-analysis-of-machine-learning-models-in-shap-analysis|machine learning]] training epochs. By visualizing the historical importance of these chips, the work provides a roadmap of how hardware breakthroughs have historically dictated the boundaries of [[triage-routing-software-engineering-tasks-to-cost-effective-llm-tiers-via-code-q|software engineering]] and the limits of [[big-data|big data]] processing.
