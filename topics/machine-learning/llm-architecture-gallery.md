# LLM Architecture Gallery

The **LLM Architecture Gallery** is a specialized visual and technical resource designed to document the structural evolution of [[large-language-models-for-outpatient-referral-problem-definition-benchmarking-an|Large Language Models]] (LLMs). Curated by Sebastian Raschka, the gallery provides a comparative examination of the architectural shifts that have occurred since the introduction of the original [[crft-consistent-recurrent-feature-flow-transformer-for-cross-modal-image-registr|Transformer]] model.

## Overview of Architectural Evolution

The gallery serves as a structural map for researchers and engineers, illustrating how different models within the [[benchmarking-deep-learning-for-future-liver-remnant-segmentation-in-colorectal-l|Deep Learning]] domain have modified the standard decoder-only framework to achieve better performance, efficiency, and scalability. By visualizing the layers of various models, the gallery highlights the transition from basic [[curvature-aware-optimization-for-high-accuracy-physics-informed-neural-networks|Neural Network]] designs to highly sophisticated, optimized engines.

Key architectural components frequently analyzed within the gallery include:

*   **[[attention-mechanism|Attention Mechanism]] Variants:** The progression from standard Multi-Head Attention to more efficient methods like Grouped-Query Attention (GQA) and Multi-Query Attention (MQA).
*   **Positional Encoding:** The shift from absolute positional embeddings to more robust methods like Rotary Positional Embeddings (RoPE).
*   **Normalization Techniques:** The move from LayerNorm to more computationally efficient alternatives like RMSNorm.
*   **Feed-Forward Networks:** Variations in the expansion ratios and activation functions used within the transformer blocks.

## Significance in the AI Landscape

As [[a-comparative-analysis-of-machine-learning-models-in-shap-analysis|Machine Learning]] research moves toward larger-scale deployments, understanding the nuances of [[decoder-only-architecture|Decoder-only Architecture]] becomes vital. The gallery tracks the lineage of influential models such as the [[forgerygpt-a-multimodal-llm-for-interpretable-image-forgery-detection-and-locali|GPT]] series, [[llama|Llama]], and [[mistral|Mistral]], making it an essential reference for anyone working in [[natural-language-processing|Natural Language Processing]] (NLP). 

By providing a side-by-side comparison of these architectures, the gallery allows practitioners to observe how specific algorithmic tweaks—aimed at increasing context window length or reducing inference latency—impact the overall computational footprint of the model. This makes it an invaluable tool for those studying the intersection of algorithm design and hardware optimization.
