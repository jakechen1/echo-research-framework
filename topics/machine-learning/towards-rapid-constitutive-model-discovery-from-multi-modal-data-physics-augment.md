# Towards Rapid Constitutive Model Discovery from Multi-Modal Data: Physics Augmented Finite Element Model Updating (paFEMU)

## Overview
The research paper introduces **physics augmented finite element model updating (paFEMU)**, a novel framework designed to accelerate the discovery of [[constitutive-models|Constitutive Models]] through the integration of heterogeneous data sources. As the field of [[material-science|Material Science]] shifts from purely data-driven paradigms toward **physics augmentation**, there is a critical need to enforce mechanistic principles while leveraging the predictive power of [[a-comparative-analysis-of-machine-learning-models-in-shap-analysis|Machine Learning]].

## The Challenge of Multi-Modal Data
Traditional constitutive modeling often relies on pre-defined phenomenological models or purely data-driven approaches. However, modern materials modeling workflows are rarely uniform; they are characterized by **multi-modal** and **multi-fidelity** data. This includes a variety of sources ranging from low-fidelity, simple mechanical testing to high-fidelity, full-field data acquisition. Effectively synthesizing these disparate data streams—often involving different materials within the same class—remains a significant hurdle in [[computational-mechanics|Computational Mechanics]].
