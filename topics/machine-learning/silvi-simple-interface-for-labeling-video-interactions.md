# SiLVi: Simple Interface for Labeling Video Interactions

SiLVi (Simple Interface for Labeling Video Interactions) is an open-source software solution designed to bridge a critical gap in automated video analysis. As [[computer-vision|computer vision]] methods become increasingly vital for processing large-scale datasets from [[camera-traps|camera traps]], [[drones]], and direct field observations, the ability to precisely annotate not just individual actions, but the complex interactions between subjects, has become essential for understanding [[social-behavior|social behavior]].

## The Annotation Gap

Traditionally, video annotation tools have been bifurcated, presenting a significant hurdle for researchers:
* **Behavioral Labeling:** Tools that allow for labeling actions but lack the spatial localization of individual subjects.
* **Localization-only Tools:** Tools that can track individuals but lack the capacity to capture and categorize inter-subject interactions.

SiLVi addresses this limitation by integrating both functionalities into a single, streamlined interface. This allows researchers to annotate behaviors and interactions simultaneously within video frames, generating the structured outputs necessary for training and validating [[a-comparative-analysis-of-machine-learning-models-in-shap-analysis|machine learning]] models.

## Applications and Impact

By linking the fields of [[behavioral-ecology|behavioral ecology]] and [[artificial-intelligence-and-the-structure-of-mathematics|artificial intelligence]], SiLVi facilitates the development of sophisticated, automated approaches for fine-grained behavioral analysis. While the software was primarily developed to assist in studying animal behavior, its utility extends to any domain requiring the extraction of [[dynamic-scene-graphs|dynamic scene graphs]] from video data. Potential applications include:
* **Human-Computer Interaction:** Analyzing complex social dynamics in human populations.
* **Robotics:** Training agents to recognize and react to environmental interactions.
* **Automated Surveillance:** Developing models for recognizing complex object-object relationships in security footage.

## Availability

The SiLVi software package, including comprehensive documentation and installation instructions, is available through the official Ecker Lab website: https://silvi.eckerlab.org.
