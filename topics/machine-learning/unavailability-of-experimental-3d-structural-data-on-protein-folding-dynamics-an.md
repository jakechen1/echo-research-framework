# Unavailability of experimental 3D structural data on protein folding dynamics

The study addresses a critical bottleneck in [[structural-biology|Structural Biology]]: the extreme scarcity of experimental data regarding [[towards-protein-folding-pathways-by-reconstructing-protein-residue-networks-with|Protein Folding]] intermediates. While the scientific community possesses abundant data for determining [[native-structure|Native Structure]], the 3D conformations of non-native, transient states—known as [[folding-intermediates|Folding Intermediates]]—remain difficult to capture using current experimental technologies.

## The Data Gap
The research highlights that analyzing these intermediates is essential for understanding [[protein-dynamics|Protein Dynamics]] and the underlying mechanisms of [[misfolding-related-diseases|Misfolding-related Diseases]]. To quantify the current state of the field, the authors conducted a literature review, identifying only six studies that provide 3D structural data on these intermediates. These studies were limited to a small number of proteins, primarily focusing on [[co-translational-folding|Co-translational Folding]] and [[post-translational-folding|Post-translational Folding]].

## Limitations of Current AI Models
A significant finding of the paper is the performance gap in existing [[a-comparative-analysis-of-machine-learning-models-in-shap-analysis|Machine Learning]]-based prediction tools. Specifically, the assessment demonstrates that [[alphafold2|AlphaFold2]], while revolutionary for predicting stable native structures, fails to accurately predict the non-native configurations present during the co-translational folding process. This limitation suggests that the "static" approach of current-generation models is insufficient for capturing the temporal evolution of a protein's shape during its synthesis.

## Future Directions
Despite these challenges, the study provides a roadmap for the next generation of [[us-cities-are-axing-flock-safety-surveillance-technology|Technology]] in the field. It identifies emerging, pioneering methods that integrate intrinsic [[biophysical-characteristics|Biophysical Characteristics]] directly into prediction algorithms. These new models are designed to account for the physics of folding dynamics, offering a promising pathway to bridge the gap between [[amino-acid-sequence|Amino Acid Sequence]] and the complex, transient landscape of the protein folding pathway.
