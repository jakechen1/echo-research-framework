# Riemann-Bench: A Benchmark for Moonshot Mathematics

The recent progress of [[artificial-intelligence-and-the-structure-of-mathematics|Artificial Intelligence]] in [[do-mllms-really-understand-space-a-mathematical-reasoning-evaluation|Mathematical Reasoning]] has been marked by models achieving gold-medal-level performance on the [[international-mathematical-olympiad|International Mathematical Olympiad]] (IMO). However, these achievements represent a narrow subset of mathematics characterized by limited domains and reliance on clever algorithmic tricks. To evaluate the next frontier of mathematical capability, researchers have introduced **Riemann-Bench**.

### Overview
Riemann-Bench is a private [[evgeoqa-benchmarking-llms-on-dynamic-multi-objective-geo-spatial-exploration|Benchmark]] consisting of 25 expert-curated problems designed to test [[a-comparative-analysis-of-machine-learning-models-in-shap-analysis|Machine Learning]] systems on research-level mathematics. The difficulty level is intentionally set far beyond the "olympiad frontier," focusing on deep theoretical knowledge rather than competition-style pattern recognition. The problem set was authored by a prestigious group, including Ivy League mathematics professors, graduate students, and PhD-holding IMO medalists, many of whom required weeks of independent work to solve the problems personally.

### Methodology and Rigor
To maintain the highest standards of scientific integrity, Riemann-Bench employs a rigorous verification pipeline:
* **Double-Blind Verification:** Each problem must be solved from scratch by two independent domain experts.
* **Automated Verification:** Every problem yields a unique, closed-form solution that is assessed using programmatic verifiers to ensure accuracy.
