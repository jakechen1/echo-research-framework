# The ATOM Report: Measuring the Open Language Model Ecosystem

The **ATOM Report** provides a comprehensive analytical snapshot of the global [[Open Language Models]] landscape. By examining approximately 1,500 mainline models, the study offers deep insights into the foundational architectures that support the modern [[Artificial Intelligence]] ecosystem. The research focuses on key players such as [[Alibaba Qwen]], [[DeepSeek]], and [[Meta Llama]], which serve as the primary building blocks for researchers, entrepreneurs, and policy-making bodies.

## Key Findings and Market Shifts

A primary focus of the report is the tracking of geopolitical trends in [[Machine Learning]] development. The data reveals a significant shift in momentum: during the summer of 2025, Chinese-developed models overtook their U.S.-based counterparts in adoption and influence. Furthermore, the report documents that this gap has continued to widen in the subsequent months, signaling a major change in the global distribution of [[Technology]] leadership.

## Methodology

To construct a holistic view of the ecosystem, the researchers utilized a multi-dimensional approach to measurement, including:

*   **[[Hugging Face]] Downloads:** Tracking the raw popularity and accessibility of model weights.
*   **Model Derivatives:** Measuring the proliferation of fine-tuned versions and specialized iterations derived from base models.
*   **[[Inference Market Share]]:** Evaluating how extensively these models are utilized in live production environments and API services.
*   **Performance Metrics:** Assessing the qualitative and quantitative capabilities of the models against established benchmarks.

## Implications

The findings presented in the ATOM Report are critical for stakeholders navigating the rapidly changing [[AI]] landscape. For entrepreneurs, understanding which models are dominating the market can inform product development. For policy advisors, the widening gap between Eastern and Western model adoption highlights the importance of monitoring the global supply chain of compute and algorithmic innovation.
