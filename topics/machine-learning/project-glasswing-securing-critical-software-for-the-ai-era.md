# Project Glasswing: Securing critical software for the AI era

## Overview
Project Glasswing is a research-driven initiative focused on the intersection of [[Software Engineering]] and [[Artificial Intelligence]] safety. As the integration of [[Large Language Models]] into the software development lifecycle becomes more pervasive, the project seeks to address the emerging security landscape where AI-driven capabilities meet the fundamental layers of digital infrastructure.

## The Challenge of the AI Era
The transition toward an era of autonomous coding introduces unprecedented risks to [[Cybersecurity]]. As [[Machine Learning]] models gain the ability to write, debug, and deploy code, the traditional boundaries of the [[Software Supply Chain]] are shifting. There is a growing concern that the speed of AI-assisted development may outpace our ability to perform rigorous [[Software Verification]], potentially introducing subtle, high-impact vulnerabilities into [[Critical Infrastructure]].

Furthermore, the potential for [[Automated Exploit Generation]]—where AI is used to discover and weaponize zero-day vulnerabilities—necessitates a proactive approach to defending the software that underpins global digital ecosystems.

## Core Research Objectives
Project Glasswing focuses on several key pillars of protection:
* **Resilient Infrastructure:** Developing methods to secure the foundational software layers that support both human and [[Artificial Intelligence]] operations.
* **Automated Defense:** Enhancing [[Automated Testing]] and [[Formal Verification]] techniques to detect flaws that might be obscured by AI-generated complexity.
* **Risk Mitigation:** Identifying the specific failure modes introduced when [[LLMs]] are granted agency over critical software components.

## Significance
By prioritizing the security of the "glasswing" layers—the transparent but vital software components that hold systems together—this initiative contributes to the broader field of [[Safety Engineering]]. Project Glasswing emphasizes that [[AI Safety]] cannot be achieved in isolation; it requires a holistic commitment to securing the broader [[Technology]] ecosystem and the code that defines it.
