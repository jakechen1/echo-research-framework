# Rethinking Residual Errors in Compensation-based LLM Quantization

The paper "Rethinking Residual Errors in Compensation-based LLM Quantization" proposes a novel optimization strategy for the [[3dturboquant-training-free-near-optimal-quantization-for-3d-reconstruction-model|Quantization]] of [[large-language-models-for-outpatient-referral-problem-definition-benchmarking-an|Large Language Models]] (LLMs). As the scale of [[curvature-aware-optimization-for-high-accuracy-physics-informed-neural-networks|Neural Networks]] continues to grow, [[enec-a-lossless-ai-model-compression-method-enabling-fast-inference-on-ascend-np|Model Compression]] techniques have become critical for deploying high-performance models on resource-constrained hardware.

### Background
Recent advancements in [[a-comparative-analysis-of-machine-learning-models-in-shap-analysis|Machine Learning]] have popularized weight compensation methods to mitigate the accuracy loss caused by low-bit quantization. Landmark techniques such as [[gptq|GPTQ]] allow for efficient-parameter quantization by iteratively adjusting weights to minimize output errors. Building on this, [[gptaq|GPTAQ]] introduced an asymmetric calibration process intended to align the output of quantized layers with their full-precision counterparts.

### The Problem: Sub-optimal Calibration
The authors identify a fundamental shortcoming in the calibration objectives of existing iterative weight compensation frameworks. Currently, during the intra-layer calibration process, these methods align the quantized output with the output generated by *compensated* weights, rather than the original [[megatrain-full-precision-training-of-100b-parameter-llms-on-a-single-gpu|Full-Precision]] model output. This divergence creates an accumulating error that deviates from the ground-truth model performance.

### Proposed Innovation: Compensation-Aware Error
To rectify this, the researchers redefine the calibration objective to ensure that the quantized model's output precisely aligns with the original output of the full-precision model at each step of the process. 

A key contribution of this work is the discovery of the **"compensation-aware error."** The authors reveal that the residual error is not solely a product of output differences from preceding layers; it also stems from the internal discrepancy between the compensated weights and the original weights within a single layer. 

### Results and Implementation
By utilizing the neuron decomposition technique inherited from [[gptaq|GPTAQ]], the authors developed a method to efficiently incorporate this compensation-aware error into the weight update cycle. Extensive benchmarks across various LLM architectures demonstrate that these enhancements are compatible with both [[gptq|GPTQ]] and [[gptaq|GPTAQ]], significantly boosting [[3dturboquant-training-free-near-optimal-quantization-for-3d-reconstruction-model|Quantization]] accuracy and performance.
