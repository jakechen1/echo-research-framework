# Instruction-Tuned LLMs for Parsing and Mining Unstructured Logs on Leadership HPC Systems

The research explores solutions for the growing complexity of managing massive, heterogeneous, and unstructured system logs generated by leadership-class [[High-Performance Computing (HPC)]] systems. As these systems integrate diverse hardware and software layers, the resulting telemetry becomes increasingly difficult to parse, hindering the ability to perform effective [[Anomaly Detection]] and system analysis.

## Overview of the Approach

To address the challenges of unstructured data, the authors introduce a domain-adapted, instruction-following framework driven by [[Large Language Models (LLMs)]]. The core of this methodology involves the use of an 8B-parameter [[LLaMA]] model that has been specifically optimized for the HPC domain. By leveraging [[Instruction Tuning]] and [[Chain-of-Thought (CoT) Reasoning]], the framework can transform raw, inconsistent log formats into highly structured data with high fidelity.

A key innovation presented is a hybrid fine-tuning methodology. This approach allows a general-purpose model to be adapted to domain-specific telemetry while maintaining a footprint small enough for local, privacy-preserving, and energy-efficient deployment. This is particularly critical for large-scale [[Supercomputing]] environments where data security and computational overhead are primary concerns.

## Experimental Results and Validation

The researchers evaluated their model using datasets from the [[LogHub]] repository, comparing its performance against much larger architectures. The results indicate that the fine-tuned 8B model achieves parsing accuracy on par with significantly larger models, including [[LLaMA 70B]] and [[Anthropic's Claude]].

Beyond synthetic testing, the utility of the model was validated using real-world production data. The authors processed over 600 million logs from the [[Frontier supercomputer]] over a four-week window. The application of the model successfully uncovered critical operational patterns, including:
* Temporal dynamics in system performance.
* Node-level anomalies.
* Correlations between specific workloads and error-log occurrences.

This work demonstrates that specialized, [[Machine Learning]]-driven parsing can provide the granular insights necessary for the reliable operation of next-generation computational infrastructure.
