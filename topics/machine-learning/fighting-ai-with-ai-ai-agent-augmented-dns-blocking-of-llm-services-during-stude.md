# Fighting AI with AI: AI-Agent Augmented DNS Blocking of LLM Services during Student Evaluations

The rapid integration of [[large-language-models-for-outpatient-referral-problem-definition-benchmarking-an|Large Language Models]] (LLMs) into the educational landscape presents a significant technological duality. While these models offer transformative potential for personalized learning and increased accessibility, they also introduce unprecedented challenges to [[academic-integrity|Academic Integrity]]. A primary concern is the rise of "cognitive offloading," where students use generative AI to bypass critical thinking processes, thereby undermining the validity of academic assessments.

## The AI-Sinkhole Framework

To combat the unauthorized use of AI during proctored examinations, researchers have introduced **AI-Sinkhole**. This is an AI-agent augmented, [[dns-based|DNS-based]] framework designed to protect the sanctity of student evaluations. The system is engineered to dynamically discover, semantically classify, and implement temporary, network-wide blocking of emerging LLM chatbot services.

### Technical Implementation

The architecture of AI-Sinkhole relies on the synergy between traditional networking tools and modern [[a-comparative-analysis-of-machine-learning-models-in-shap-analysis|Machine Learning]] models:

*   **DNS-Based Filtering:** The framework utilizes [[pi-hole|Pi-Hole]] for efficient, network-wide traffic management and blocking.
*   **Semantic Classification:** Unlike static blacklists that fail to recognize new domains, AI-Sinkhole employs quantized [[automating-database-native-function-code-synthesis-with-llms|LLMs]]—including [[llama-3|Llama 3]], [[deepseek-r1|DeepSeek-R1]], and [[qwen-3|Qwen-3]]—to identify and classify services based on their semantic footprint.
*   **Explainability:** By leveraging LLMs as classifiers, the system provides explainable classification results, allowing administrators to understand the rationale behind blocked services.

### Performance and Results

Experimental observations indicate that using LLMs as explainable classifiers yields highly robust, cross-lingual performance. The framework achieved an [[f1-score|F1-score]] of greater than 0.83, demonstrating its ability to function effectively across different linguistic contexts and emerging service architectures.

## Future Research

AI-Sinkhole serves as a foundation for developing adaptive [[rpm-net-reciprocal-point-mlp-network-for-unknown-network-security-threat-detecti|Network Security]] protocols in educational environments.
